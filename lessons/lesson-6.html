Lesson 6: Showcase & Reflection

45–60 minutes Windows PC, Browser

Today you will…

  • Present your AI product to the class (or a partner)
  • Reflect on what you have learned across the whole unit
  • Discuss what AI can and cannot do ("Mythbusters" ethics activity)

Equipment Needed

  • Windows PC with browser (to show your Teachable Machine model)
  • Your completed product from Lesson 5

Key Vocabulary

EthicsThinking about what is right and wrong — should we use AI for everything?
ReflectionLooking back at what you learned and how you could improve.
ShowcasePresenting your work to an audience to explain what you made and how it works.

Starter Activity (5 minutes)

Quick fire: Without looking back, write down as many key words from this unit as you can remember in 2 minutes.

Target words: machine learning, model, class/label, training data, testing data, accuracy, prediction, bias, fairness, overfitting, confidence, balanced dataset. Award points for each correct keyword.

Main Tasks

Part A: Showcase Your Product (20 minutes)

Presentation Guide

Present your Lesson 5 product to your partner or the class. Cover these points:

  1. Product name and what problem it solves
  2. Who is the target user?
  3. Demo: Show your model working on Teachable Machine
  4. Accuracy: How well does it work? Show your results.
  5. Improvements: What would you change if you had more time?

After presenting, get feedback from your partner or the class:

Organisation options:

  • Pairs: Students present to a partner, then swap. Each gives 2 stars and a wish.
  • Gallery walk: Students set up their model on screen, class walks around and tries each one.
  • Whole class: Select 3–4 volunteers to present to everyone. Use a timer (3 mins each).

Part B: AI Mythbusters (15 minutes)

For each statement, decide: is it TRUE or FALSE? Write your answer and explain why.

Statement True or False? Why?
"AI can think and feel like a human."
"AI is always fair and unbiased."
"AI learns from data — if the data is bad, the AI is bad."
"AI will replace all human jobs."
"AI needs humans to create and check it."

Answers:

  1. FALSE — AI doesn't have feelings, consciousness, or understanding. It follows patterns in data.
  2. FALSE — AI can be biased if trained on biased data (as we saw in Lesson 2).
  3. TRUE — This is the key lesson from the unit. "Garbage in, garbage out."
  4. FALSE (mostly) — AI will change many jobs but not replace all of them. Jobs requiring creativity, empathy, and complex judgement are harder to automate.
  5. TRUE — Humans create AI, choose the training data, and need to check it works properly and fairly.

Part C: Unit Reflection

Looking Back at the Unit

Common Mistakes & Quick Fixes

  • Presentation too short: Make sure you cover all 5 points in the presentation guide above.
  • Can't remember model results: Check your Lesson 5 page — your answers are saved!
  • Mythbusters too easy: Discuss in pairs first, then as a class. Encourage debate!

Challenge / Stretch Task

Write a short paragraph (4–5 sentences) explaining to a Year 6 student what machine learning is and why understanding bias matters. Use simple language they would understand.

Exit Ticket

Expected answers:

  1. Examples: medical diagnosis, sorting recycling, helping blind people navigate, detecting wildfires.
  2. Examples: bias/discrimination, privacy concerns, job losses, deepfakes, over-reliance on technology.
  3. Because AI can be biased, make mistakes, or be used in harmful ways. Humans need to make sure AI is fair, accurate, and used responsibly.
  4. Self-assessment — look for students who can justify their rating.
